Machine tokenization has moved from experiment to production. Revenue-generating equipment (farms, robots, logistics assets, compute nodes) is increasingly represented onchain, with verified owners and programmable revenue sharing.

That shift changed the question. Teams no longer ask whether a machine *can* be tokenized. They ask how to do it **compliantly**, **reproducibly**, and **at scale**.

The **Arbitrum Machine RWA Framework** answers that with a standardized onchain structure and a single SDK (`arbitrum-machine-rwa-sdk`) that covers the full lifecycle, or any slice of it you need.

---

## From balance sheets to liquid infrastructure

When machines become tradable units instead of opaque line items, finance connects directly to physical output.

Onchain participants (people, companies, protocols, and increasingly autonomous agents) can hold assets, route capital, and coordinate around **productive capacity**, not just abstract tokens.

Markets are getting comfortable with structured ownership of real equipment: fractional shares, performance-linked yield, and compliance-gated transfers. The next step is treating **machines themselves** as continuously financial objects (collateral, income streams, and portfolio building blocks), not one-off capex events.

That opens design space integrators are already exploring:

- Capital allocated across fleets based on utilization and yield
- Machine-backed shares used as collateral in lending markets
- Revenue reinvested into expansion or diversified machine portfolios
- Secondary pricing at the **device** level, not only the corporate level
- Insurance and risk products tied to specific assets and uptime

The economic unit can shift from the company to the machine, with yield tied to what the asset actually produces.

## How this framework works

The Arbitrum Machine RWA Framework gives each machine a **standardized onchain financial structure**:

1. **Identity**: ONCHAINID links wallets to verified participants (KYC topic **666**).
2. **Registration**: Machine NFTs carry DID metadata; Contract NFTs encode multi-party agreements.
3. **Tokenization container**: An **Arb Vault** holds collateral and mints **ERC-3643 (T-REX)** security tokens.
4. **Revenue routing**: A reward distributor allocates yield pro-rata to token holders.
5. **Compliance**: Transfers and minting respect the vault Identity Registry; only verified addresses participate.

A machine is registered with identity and metadata so it can be referenced consistently across apps. Operational and legal context (leasing, financing, operating agreements) attaches via **Contract NFTs** and off-chain documents hashed onchain.

The Machine NFT is deposited into the vault and fractionalized into compliant ownership shares. Revenue from operations can be deposited and claimed according to ownership logic.

Subject to your jurisdiction and licensing, issuers may also structure **pre-operational financing**: capital raised against a defined machine profile, with the NFT minted and linked when the asset goes live.

### Roles in the lifecycle

The framework separates responsibilities so real-world participants plug into the same pipeline:

| Role | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Framework Owner** | Deploys core contracts, vault factory, trusted issuers |
| **Machine Regulator** | Authorizes machine issuers |
| **Machine Issuer** | Registers machines and assigns NFTs to owners |
| **Claim Issuer** | Verifies identities and signs KYC claims |
| **Vault controller** | Deposits collateral, mints tokens, may deposit yield |
| **Investors** | Hold verified shares, receive transfers and yield |
| **Integrators / agents** | Build apps and automations on top of the SDK |

The **technical shape is fixed**; **financing models stay flexible**; leasing, revenue share, fixed-yield structures, and future instrument designs can sit on the same primitives.

## Built for compliance by design

Machines and robots are infrastructure: energy, mobility, production, compute. Markets around them need **trust at the asset layer**, not only at the exchange layer.

This framework embeds compliance into the token itself:

- **ONCHAINID** for participant identity
- **Trusted Claim Issuers** for KYC and role claims
- **ERC-3643 Identity Registry** per vault: transfers blocked for unverified wallets
- **Auditable collateral**: Machine and Contract NFTs locked in vaults with onchain ownership history

Regulators, marketplaces, and compliance providers can reason about **the same machine record** across venues. Jurisdiction-specific rules attach via claims, issuers, and off-chain policy, without rewriting the core contract architecture.

Your legal and licensing obligations remain yours to implement; the stack gives you **hooks and enforcement points** on Arbitrum.

## A different growth model

Machine RWA does not replace operating companies. It changes **how they fund growth**.

Instead of raising equity to buy every device upfront:

- **Fleet expansion** can be financed per machine or per vault
- **Usage-based ownership** replaces all-or-nothing equipment sales
- **Performance-linked yield** aligns investors with output, not only equity stories
- **Secondary liquidity** can apply to operational assets, not just company shares

Companies keep operational control; machines gain a standardized path to capital and distribution.
